Three directions for your font

Same letter skeletons, three personalities — pick the one that feels like you.
These are not stock fonts — every glyph below was generated a few minutes ago by the parametric engine that will build your final font. Whichever direction you pick is literally what gets refined into the full character set (A–Z, a–z, 0–9, all symbols, Bold + Italic, coding ligatures).
Option A

Sharp & Angular

45° cut corners, octagonal rounds — sci-fi terminal energy, edges like volcanic glass. Distinctive in headings, still calm enough for long notes.

Option B

Retro Terminal

Square corners, heavy strokes — chunky DOS/CRT nostalgia. Loudest of the three; great for headers and terminals, heavier as body text.

Option C

Clean & Minimal

Rounded geometric forms — quiet and highly readable, closer to a classic coding font. The tech personality lives in details: slashed zero, square dots, angular tilde.
